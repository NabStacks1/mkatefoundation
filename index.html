In loving memory of Madam Kate Amosun. Celebrating her exemplary existence, entrepreneurship skill and her advocating for women affairs. Carrying on her legacy spreading wealth and a better economy.

(EMPOWERING WOMEN IN BUSINESS)

BIOGRAPHY

Mrs. Kate Ajoke Amosun (fondly called IyaGhana). She was born into the Amparberg family on December 18th 1941in Akin Oda,Eastern Region Ghana. She is Ashanti by tribe, a native of Wenchi near Kumasi. She started her primary education at the united girls school, proceeded to Happy Girls School, akin Oda to further her education in catering and sewing.



MARRIAGE

With her father wanting her to marry a Nigerian and her Mother objecting, she eventually got married to Late Chief Hezekiah Amosun in 1958. After her modern school as it was called in those days,she was the third wife and her Husband gave her the Yoruba name and oriki of his family, Ola’s compound Ipee, Ajoke Abe Amosun. Knowing how difficult Ghana’s economy was then, even after leaving Ghana for Nigeria in 1969 with her husband, she would go to see her family members every year. She brought siblings, nephew,nieces,and cousins to Nigeria and while in Lagos they lived and worked with her.
Accommodating her step children also, shetraded alongside her immediate senior wife Mrs. Elizabeth Amosun before moving to Nigeria.No one could tell the difference between her children and step children. Their success can be traced to the impact she had in their lives.



BUSINESS CAREER

Her husband established the then Apapa super market in 1970. Three of the wives worked together in the supermarket. During this period, she served diligently to boost and securethebusiness of her Husband. As she was running the supermarket she also looked after her personalshop at EbuteMetta.
She moved to Ilorin in 1980 to establish the once Famous“Amosun Store” at Unity Road in Ilorin. She manned the affairs of the supermarket for almost 20years. She established the Amosun snack bar, doing different kinds of snack. Her Integrity, kind heart, and diligence boosted the success of the supermarket which eventually led to its fame in Kwara state.
After the death of her husband in 1993, she resigned from the supermarket in 1996 leaving the Amosun children to take over the affairs of the super market. In 1997 she started her “Kate Ghana bread” with her brother which was a huge success, being a very good caterer and seamstress; she would bake and make Christmasdresses for her children, A very industrious and hardworking woman.
She accommodated so many teenagers, trained young boys and girls in catering, leaving them with her teachings which some is known for now. Well known among the Ghanaians in Ilorin then, she was always ready to accommodate and help them anytime as most of them worked in Amosun Stores.
The Amosun Supermarket in her care has created opportunities, and contributed immensely to the economic growth of the city of Ilorin and Kwara State.



SERVICE TO GOD

Aside her diligence in business, she was a devoted Christian too, coming back from Ghana she decided to serve God as she stayed with her children, she remained active in the Emmanuel Baptist Church Sabo-Oke, Ilorin for many years till it became impossible for her to partake in church activities again. She was a member of 12 disciples and other Evangelical ministries, to be noted she was a member of the WMU (Women Missionary Union) also a member of the Breakfast Disciples who see to the welfare of widows and orphans.
She was a Humanitarian, a Lover of God and a Mother to many. Touching as much life as she could through Christ and serving humanity with kindness and helping others grow and learn under her service, living an exemplary life she impacted others with her kind heart.

Our Story

How it started

In memory of the late Mrs.Kate Amosun mother of Engr. Job Amosun, Director of Virtual Global Impressions, celebrating her exemplary existence and strive in entrepreneurship and advocating for women affairs. The management, Kwara Live awards established a category in the honor in 2017.
Forerun, the management through this category hence awarded outstanding female business personalities for their impact and excellence in the likes of Mrs. JoyAbolarin {Proprietress of Jibore Immaculate School} 2017, Mrs. Adenike Adewunmi {Director of G-Pinnacle} 2018, Dr. Morountodun Joseph {Eja Osan foundation} 2019 and Miss Boluwatife Marie 2020.
Championing development, the management Kwara live project has resulted to empowering younger work force for a better livelihood, spreading wealth and a better economy.

Our Mission

The What and Why

Women’s empowerment has emerged as a part of a major global movement and is continuing to break new ground in recent years. Empowering women is essential to the health and social development of families, communities, and countries. When women are living safe, fulfilled and productive lives, they can reach their full potential. Contributing their skills to the workforce and can raise happier and healthier children. They are also able to help fuel sustainable economies and benefit societies and humanity at large. Women empowerment in Nigeria is an economic process that involves empowering Nigerian women as a poverty reduction measure. Empowerment is the development of women in terms of politics, social and economic strength in nation development. It is also a way of reducing women’s vulnerability, and dependency in all spheres of life. It can be noted that the aggregate of educational, political, health and legal empowerment are key to women’s empowerment in Nigeria.
